Development Economics · Education · Labor Economics

I am a development economist with research interests in education, labor economics, and public policy in low- and middle-income countries.

I am currently a Research Fellow at J-PAL Europe (Paris School of Economics), where I contribute to research on education and social inclusion, with ongoing field experiments in Uzbekistan. I hold a PhD in Economics from Central European University and previously held a Visiting Research Fellowship at the Center for International Development, Harvard Kennedy School.

My research uses experimental and quasi-experimental methods to study how information interventions and policy design shape educational outcomes and labor market behavior, with applications to Central Asia and other low- and middle-income contexts.
